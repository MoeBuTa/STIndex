EMERGENCY WEATHER REPORT — Bureau of Meteorology Australia
Issued: 9 April 2021, 06:00 AWST

TROPICAL CYCLONE SEROJA — IMPACT SUMMARY

Tropical Cyclone Seroja formed in the Timor Sea on 5 April 2021 and rapidly
intensified into a Category 3 system by 8 April 2021. The cyclone tracked
south-southeast before making landfall near Kalbarri, Western Australia at
approximately 8:00 PM AWST on 11 April 2021.

AFFECTED REGIONS:
- Kalbarri (WA): Direct landfall. Gusts exceeding 170 km/h recorded at
  Kalbarri Airport at 20:15 AWST on 11 April 2021.
- Geraldton (WA): Severe damage to marina and coastal infrastructure.
  Flooding reported from 10 PM on 11 April through 2 AM on 12 April 2021.
- Northampton (WA): Power outages from midnight 11 April until 14 April 2021.
- Perth (WA): Remnant rainfall and strong winds on 13 April 2021.

TIMELINE:
  5 Apr 2021  — Tropical low develops 400 km north of Darwin, Northern Territory
  7 Apr 2021  — Upgraded to Category 1 cyclone near Kupang, Indonesia
  8 Apr 2021  — Rapid intensification; Category 3 north of Broome
  11 Apr 2021 — Landfall at Kalbarri at Category 3 intensity
  12 Apr 2021 — Weakens to tropical low inland near Morawa
  13 Apr 2021 — Remnants dissipate over the Goldfields region

DAMAGE ASSESSMENT (as of 15 April 2021):
Estimated damage across the Midwest Gascoyne region of Western Australia
exceeded AUD 1.2 billion. The coastal town of Kalbarri sustained the most
severe structural damage, with over 70% of buildings requiring repairs.

Emergency relief centres were established in Geraldton on 12 April 2021 and
in Northampton on 13 April 2021. The Australian Defence Force deployed to
Kalbarri on 14 April 2021 to assist with recovery operations.

For further updates contact the Bureau of Meteorology, Perth Office.
